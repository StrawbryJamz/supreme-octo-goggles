Question 2: In what ways does this issue impact society in general?

Psychological suffering. Children are exposed to situations of terror and horror during war – experiences that may leave enduring impacts in posttraumatic stress disorder. Severe losses and disruptions in their lives lead to high rates of depression and anxiety in war-affected children. Also Abduction’s leaves a high amount of stress on the family that can result in abuse or job loss.

Question 3: What are the major ways this issue is being addressed, give at least 3 specific examples with a detailed explanation for each?

    1. A way that this issue is trying to be addressed is during 1949 by the Geneva Conventions which states that all parties in a conflict shall take “all feasible measures” to ensure that children under the age of 15 do not direct action in a war.

    2. Many countries like Australia, Germany, Belgium and many other countries have introduced a law that states that recruiting or using children under 15 years old, criminally liable.

    3. The country that has done the most consideration with the issue is the United States they have not only had laws but have also made it that such people are ineligible for immigration status, and they cannot seek asylum. The United States has also passed an act that prevents military aid to countries that exploit children. With the only way, aid can be provided to those countries is if the president overrides this on a basis of national interest.

    4. The United Nations have also addressed the issue with many ‘Actions Plans’ which outline a list of steps that have been taken to stop child soldiers. The rules that these action plans enforce are they criminalize the recruitment and the use of child soldiers, they issue a military to stop recruitment, appointing child protection specialist in security forces and strengthening birth registration and integrating age-verification mechanisms in the recruitment process.

Question 4: Provide thoughts from two Secular sources or scholars on the issue?

The United Nations View on Child Soldiers

Margot Wallström , Minister for Foreign Affairs of Sweden and Leila Zerrougui , Special Representative of the Secretary-General for Children and Armed Conflict says that:

“When kids close their eyes, they do not replay the happy and innocent memories of their day, but instead fight to keep from their minds the horrors they have seen that day. There is no loving parent to tuck them in; indeed, many shudder at the sound of footsteps approaching their bed. This is the experience of child soldiers around the world.Since 2000, over 115,000 children have been released from armed forces and groups. This shows the power of the UN and member states working together to give back a future to those who thought they had been abandoned.” “No child should ever have to pick up a weapon and fight” said Mahimbo Mdoe, the head of UNICEF programmes in the country."

Question 5: Which United Nation Declaration(s) of Human Rights is/are related to this issue? Explain how it/they is/are related?

One of the United Nation Declaration of Human Rights that supports this issue is the UN Convention on the Rights of the Child which is a declaration that covers over 54 articles. This declaration was founded on the 20th of November, 1989 with the idea coming from Savethechildren founder Eglantyne Jebb, in 1923. Around 194 countries signing the delcaration with the only ones not signing being Somalia, South Sudan, and the United States.
